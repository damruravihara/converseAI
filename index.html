Project Scope

Introduction

In the ever-evolving landscape of talent acquisition, the traditional interview process has encountered remarkable transformations, aligning itself with the possibilities unleashed by modern technology. Amid this transformation, our collaborative team has undertaken the ambitious endeavor of developing an innovative Interview Bot system, pioneering a paradigm shift in the way interviews are conducted and candidates are evaluated. This system harmoniously combines sophisticated technologies encompassing speech recognition, sentiment analysis, facial authentication, and an adaptable question bank to revolutionize the recruitment experience.

The Interview Bot project represents the embodiment of modernization in human resource management, addressing the challenges faced by organizations in identifying and selecting the best-fit talent. The quest began with meticulous research and an exploration of the capabilities that cutting-edge technologies, such as deep learning, natural language processing, and user authentication, could offer to the recruitment domain.

Our primary goal was to create an intelligent, adaptable, and reliable interview bot. One that evaluates them fairly while upholding stringent rules and protocols. What ensued was a dynamic blend of human and artificial intelligence, culminating in the Interview Bot system.

This report embarks on a comprehensive exploration of the system’s core components and their associated functionalities, and how each aspect contributes to the system's effective and innovative character. Each component, namely speech recognition, facial recognition, intelligent grading system, and the dynamic question bank, is examined in-depth to unveil its role and the benefits it delivers to the holistic system.

Diving deeper, the methodology behind the development and testing of each component is meticulously presented. This section serves as a testament to our commitment to rigor and precision, showcasing the science and artistry behind our system’s construction.

The subsequent sections house our empirical findings, articulating the system's performance and reliability based on extensive testing, comparison, and analysis. Moreover, a comprehensive discussion of the results elaborates on the implications for organizations seeking to streamline their recruitment processes and select candidates accurately.

In conclusion, our Interview Bot system symbolizes a remarkable juncture in the marriage of technological innovation and human resource management. It is more than a tool; it’s a testament to how far we have come in harnessing artificial intelligence for improving recruitment efficiency. Our system brings efficiency, accuracy, and authenticity to an otherwise intricate process.

Literature Survey

The ever-evolving landscape of technology and its applications in various domains has been the subject of extensive research. In this context, we embark on a journey through a series of research papers,

"Building and Evaluating Interview Chatbots with Active Listening Skills" by M. X. Z. C. Y. C. Ziang Xiao (2020) [5], the authors focus on enhancing interview chatbots with active listening skills. The study demonstrates that incorporating active listening skills improves user engagement and elicits higher-quality responses from users.

Smith (2018), in "Advancements in Speech Recognition for Interview Bots," laid the groundwork for technology's potential in automated grading. The accurate conversion of spoken words into text is not only crucial for understanding candidates but also for evaluating the correctness and clarity of their responses. The paper emphasizes the need for speech recognition systems to capture candidates' responses accurately, which is a prerequisite for the intelligent grading system.

Research by Klappenecker, Lee, and Welch (2014) titled "Finding Available Parking Spaces Made Easy" demonstrates the evolution of speech recognition. While their work focuses on a different application (parking space detection), it highlights the increasing accuracy and real-time capabilities of speech recognition systems. This is particularly relevant to the interview bot, as real-time processing of candidate responses can provide a dynamic and engaging interview experience.

Oosterhof and Todorov (2008) explored the relationship between facial characteristics and social evaluations. While not directly related to interview bots, this research is foundational. It shows how specific facial features can influence how individuals are perceived, emphasizing the significance of facial recognition technology in making accurate identity verifications.

Xiao et al. (2020), In their research, titled "If I Hear You Correctly: Building and Evaluating Interview Chatbots with Active Listening Skills," Xiao and his team explore enhancing interview chatbots with active listening skills. While their primary emphasis is on listening skills, the implicit significance of asking pertinent follow-up questions based on user responses aligns with the dynamic question bank's purpose

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Grading System

Our interview bot uses advanced NLP and sentiment analysis, along with job-specific criteria, for fair and holistic candidate grading, taking into account both content and emotional context.

Speech Recognition

Speech recognition tech is key for modern interview bots. Our system uses advanced methods, including NLP and sentiment analysis, to transcribe and analyze speech, assessing both content and emotional context to evaluate candidate communication skills.

Facial Authentication

Our system's security relies on facial authentication, minimizing impersonation risk by comparing candidate images with official records and monitoring expressions during interviews, surpassing traditional user authentication methods.

Dynamic Question Bank

In interview bot development, dynamic question banks are an underexplored area. Current question banks are often static, lacking adaptability to candidate responses. There's a need for more interactive and personalized question banks.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

Current hiring practices demand extensive time commitments from hiring managers, often averaging 14 hours a week sourcing candidates for a single role. The integration of automation into recruitment processes represents a promising solution. Past limitations of chatbots in offering only pre-programmed responses have significantly shifted with the advent of AI. This research aims to utilize Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing technologies to streamline and enhance the hiring process.

The transition from sifting through numerous candidates to efficiently identifying the ideal fit for a role poses a demanding and labor-intensive task, particularly amidst pandemic-related constraints. The interview bot, powered by Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing techniques, proves invaluable in conducting online interviews and supporting the assessment center process. By employing sentiment analysis, the bot enhances evaluations, reducing human bias and ensuring fair assessments. This integrated approach improves efficiency, saves resources, and promotes a more objective recruitment process.

Proposed Solution

Coco Remedy uses mobile and web-based software to manage Weligama Coconut Leaf Wilt Disease (WCLWD) and Coconut Caterpillar Infestation (CCI), which devastate coconut cultivation. In WCLWD, yellowing of leaves which also associates with Magnesium deficiency makes it difficult to distinguish the diseased palms accurately. Similarly, the dried appearance of leaves due to CCI is difficult to distinguish from leaf scorching. Therefore, Deep Learning techniques like Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) for feature extraction will be used to identify the diseases accurately. Coconut growers, Estate managers of plantation companies, Researches of Coconut Research Institute (CRISL), Coconut Development Officers (CDOs) of Coconut Cultivation Board (CCB), and the general public will be able to identify diseased palm accurately by capturing a photograph of leaf with symptoms. The location will be extracted by Geo Tags in order to gather anonymous data using OpenWeatherMapAPI for future predictions. The severity of WCLWD is calculated based on the multiple symptoms using CNN. Since caterpillars’ associates with the damaged leaves in CCI, counts of caterpillars are taken using OpenCV / YOLO object detection while the infected area is calculated using MASK R-CNN. Using the above-mentioned parameters progression level is determined. Real-time notifications are sent whenever an infected tree is found. Details of the infected trees, location and severity records enable the researches and CDOs to take remedial actions. Infected areas and danger zones which might be affected in the future can visualized through Google mapping technologies. Nearby users are notified to take precautions before their lands get affected.

Research Objectives

Enhanced Candidate Interaction and Analysis

The Facial Recognition component of the Interview Bot aims to revolutionize the way candidates engage during the interview. It's designed to capture and interpret facial expressions, tone, and non-verbal cues to provide insights into candidate emotions and reactions. The primary goal is to facilitate a more engaging and comprehensive interview experience, ensuring that the candidate's non-verbal communication is considered along with their verbal responses. By incorporating this component, the Interview Bot seeks to comprehend the candidate beyond just words, thereby making the evaluation process more robust and insightful.

Accurate Speech-to-Text Transcription and Analysis

Speech Recognition forms a core component of the Interview Bot, aiming to convert spoken language into text format accurately. The goal here is to ensure precise transcription of candidate responses, enabling easy analysis and evaluation by the system. The focus is on capturing not just the words but also the sentiment and context conveyed through speech. It aims to enhance the accuracy of transcribed responses, allowing for better analysis, keyword detection, and interpretation of the candidate's communication. The objective is to significantly improve the Interview Bot's capability to understand and process verbal input effectively.

Customized and Adaptive Question Bank

The Dynamic Question Bank serves to revolutionize the conventional practice of using a static set of interview questions. It aims to introduce adaptive questioning by tailoring the interview questions to the candidate's responses, thus dynamically adjusting the difficulty level and relevance of the questions based on candidate performance. The objective is to create a personalized interview experience that is both challenging and relevant to the candidate's skills and qualifications. By offering a more customized and adaptive questioning process, the Interview Bot endeavors to provide a comprehensive and tailored evaluation of the candidates.

Intelligent Grading and Assessment System

The Intelligent Grading System focuses on evaluating candidates based on an algorithmic scoring mechanism. This component aims to assign scores based on a comprehensive evaluation of factors such as the candidate's responses, sentiment analysis, facial expressions, and the appropriateness of responses compared to pre-set benchmarks. The primary objective is to provide an unbiased, structured, and consistent scoring system. This system aims to minimize subjectivity, standardize the evaluation process, and provide a data-driven assessment that complements human judgment.

Methodology

Figure 1. High Level Architecture of the system.

The methodology for the Interview Bot system involves a comprehensive approach, encompassing various components and technological methodologies. Here is an outline for the methodology:

  1. System Design and Framework

    2. The initial phase involves designing the Interview Bot's architecture, divided into components like Speech Recognition, Sentiment Analysis, Facial Recognition, and Dynamic Question Bank. The framework is designed to enable seamless interaction between these components, ensuring smooth data flow and integration.

  2. Speech Recognition

    3. This component captures candidate responses by employing advanced speech recognition algorithms. It accurately transcribes and interprets verbal inputs using natural language processing and machine learning techniques to understand different accents, languages, and speech patterns.

  3. Sentiment Analysis

    4. Candidate sentiment during interviews is assessed using sentiment analysis techniques. Natural language processing models categorize emotions and tones in responses, with machine learning algorithms detecting positive, negative, or neutral sentiments. This involves training the system to recognize emotional nuances in spoken language.

  4. Facial Recognition

    5. Facial recognition technology aims to decipher candidates' emotional expressions and non-verbal cues, employing computer vision and machine learning techniques. These include feature extraction, facial landmark detection, and deep learning models for recognizing emotions like happiness, confusion, and stress.

  5. Dynamic Question Bank

    6. The Interview Bot uses machine learning and natural language processing to create job-specific questions and adapts them based on candidate responses and job profiles. It continuously refines its question bank through candidate interactions and interview outcomes.

  6. . Data Collection and Training

    7. The system needs a large dataset for training and fine-tuning its algorithms, including interview recordings, facial expressions, sentiment annotations, and question-answer pairs. It uses supervised learning with labeled datasets for sentiment analysis and facial recognition.

  7. System Testing and Validation

    8. The Interview Bot is rigorously tested for accuracy, reliability, and efficiency using simulated interviews, real candidate interactions, and scenario-based validations. This validation covers speech recognition, sentiment analysis, facial recognition, and question adaptation accuracy.
